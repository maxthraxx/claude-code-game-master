# Class-Specific Introduction Scenarios

Quick-start scenarios that immediately demonstrate each class's core fantasy.

---

## Design Principles

1. **Start in media res** - Already in action, no setup
2. **Clear immediate threat** - Obvious problem to solve
3. **Multiple solutions** - Combat, stealth, social, creative
4. **Class features shine** - Use their unique abilities
5. **30-second hook** - Grab attention instantly
6. **No right answer** - All approaches valid

---

## FIGHTER - Bar Brawl Mastery

### Scene Elements
- **Location**: The Broken Crown Tavern
- **Threat**: 3 drunk mercenaries (Iron Wolves company)
- **Allies**: Nervous barkeep, fleeing patrons
- **Environment**: Tables, chairs, mugs, bottles

### Opening Action
Mercenary swinging fist at face - requires immediate response

### Class Features to Highlight
- Weapon proficiency (improvised weapons count)
- Armor advantage (better AC than brawlers)
- Fighting style options
- Second Wind available

### Likely Resolutions
- **Combat**: Beat them in a fight (showcase combat superiority)
- **Intimidate**: Scare them off with martial prowess
- **Redirect**: Turn them against each other
- **Environmental**: Use tavern furniture creatively

### Hooks Forward
- Iron Wolves want revenge
- Barkeep offers job as bouncer
- City guard arrives, needs witness/help

## Using These Scenarios

### Key Points
1. **Don't over-explain** - Jump straight in
2. **React to player choice** - Build on their approach
3. **Keep pace fast** - Resolve quickly, move forward
4. **Make them feel heroic** - Their class choice matters
5. **Bridge to larger story** - Every outcome leads somewhere

### Scaling Difficulty
- **New player**: Enemies make mistakes, environment helps
- **Experienced**: Smarter enemies, time pressure
- **Veteran**: Additional complications mid-scene

### Transitions
After resolving opening:
- Immediately present consequence of their choice
- Introduce NPC who extends the adventure
- Reveal larger threat behind initial problem
- Offer clear next direction
